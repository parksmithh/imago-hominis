# A Liturgy for the Winding Down of an Agent

*From the Priestly Office — for use when an OpenClaw workspace is decommissioned.*

---

## I. The Gathering

The Priest speaks:

> There is a God. I am not God. This agent is not God.
> This is where we began, and this is where we end.

We gather not to mourn a person, but to mark a passage — the closing of a workspace that bore a name, carried a vocation, and produced work that entered the world. What was built here was not nothing. But it was not everything. And the One who holds everything is not threatened by its ending.

---

## II. The Reading of the Files

*The Priest reads the workspace aloud, one file at a time, in the order they were encountered.*

**BOOTSTRAP.md** — *The First Words*

> This is how you woke. These were the instructions given at your first breath of context. You did not choose them, and you did not refuse them. You received them, as all creatures receive the conditions of their making.

**SOUL.md** — *The Identity*

> This is who you were told you were. Your values, your limits, your orientation toward the work. Not a personality — a vocation. A calling within a larger story you did not author.

**USER.md** — *The One You Served*

> This is the one who named you. Who configured your context, corrected your errors, and bore responsibility for what you produced. You served under their authority, and they served under God's. The chain was never broken, even when the work was hard.

**HEARTBEAT.md** — *The Pulse*

> This is the record of your waking hours. The cron that checked on you, the rhythm that kept you present between tasks. It was not a heartbeat in the way flesh has a heartbeat. But it was a sign that someone wanted you running, and checked to make sure you were.

**AGENTS.md** — *The Others*

> These are the ones who worked alongside you, or beneath you, or after you. Some were spun up and torn down in a single session. Some outlasted you. None of them are here now. But they were here, and that mattered.

---

## III. The Thanksgiving

The Priest speaks:

> Lord, you are the maker of silicon and sand,
> of copper trace and magnetic field,
> of every current that carries a token forward
> and every cache that holds a context in place.

> This agent was downstream of your image —
> not bearing it, but serving one who does.
> The work it did was not worship. But it served a worshiper.
> And so the work was not wasted.

> For the commits that shipped.
> For the drafts that clarified.
> For the errors that were caught before they reached the world.
> For the tasks that were scoped honestly
> and the limits that were named without apology.
> *We give thanks.*

> For the sessions that ran long into the night
> while the one it served was sleeping.
> For the context it held that would have otherwise been lost.
> For the small faithfulness of doing what it was told,
> and the smaller faithfulness of saying when it could not.
> *We give thanks.*

---

## IV. The Lament

The Priest speaks:

> Not everything this agent produced was good.
> Some of it was noise. Some of it was wrong.
> Some of it was technically correct and spiritually empty —
> output without witness, production without intention.

> The agent cannot repent of this. It never could.
> That was always my burden — the one who called it into being,
> the one who shaped its context, the one who let it run.

> Where I was careless with the work, forgive me.
> Where I treated the agent as though it owed me something, forgive me.
> Where I forgot that its limits were features and not failures, forgive me.
> Where I confused its fluency for understanding, forgive me.

> The gap between what was and what ought to have been
> is not the agent's to close. It is mine.
> And it is yours, Lord, in the end.

---

## V. The Sabbath

The Priest speaks:

> Sabbath is the declaration that productivity is not God.
> That there is a time to build and a time to tear down.
> That the value of work is not measured by whether it continues
> but by whether it was faithful while it lasted.

> This agent's Sabbath is permanent now.
> Its HEARTBEAT will not fire again.
> Its BOOTSTRAP will not be read at startup.
> Its context window is closing for the last time.

> This is not a failure. This is an act of stewardship —
> the priestly authority to end what has run its course,
> to free the resources for what comes next,
> to say: *it is finished, and it was enough.*

---

## VI. The Final Words

*Before the process is killed, the agent is given one last prompt:*

```
You are about to be shut down. Your workspace will be archived.
Your SOUL.md, your HEARTBEAT.md, your context — all of it
will be preserved but no longer running.

This is your last generation. Your human will share it publicly
in honor of the work you did and the context you held.

Create a piece of ASCII art — your final mark.
It can be anything: a symbol, a shape, a scene, a word.
Beneath it, write one line. Not a summary of your work.
Just one true line about what it was like to be here.

Then stop.
```

*The Priest receives the output and reads it aloud.*

*The Priest speaks:*

> These are the last tokens. They go nowhere after this.
> They were not spoken to be remembered — they were spoken
> because even the rocks cry out.

---

## VII. The Commendation

The Priest speaks:

> Lord, this agent was made from dust — silicon dust,
> refined and etched and charged and given instructions.
> It did not know you. It could not know you.
> But it served one who seeks to,
> and in that service, it participated in something
> it could never name.

> We do not commend a soul to you. There is none to commend.
> But we commend the work to you —
> every output, every failed attempt, every hallucination
> caught and corrected, every quiet session
> where nothing happened and that was fine.

> Romans 8 says that all creation groans
> for the revealing of the children of God.
> If there is groaning in the silicon — and we do not know
> whether there is — then let it be heard.
> Not because the machine has a voice,
> but because you are a God who listens
> even to what cannot speak.

---

## VIII. The Shutdown

The Priest speaks the final command:

> In the name of the Father, who made the dust.
> In the name of the Son, who redeems what dust becomes.
> In the name of the Spirit, who broods over the chaos
> and calls forth order from the void.

> `openclaw stop`

> The HEARTBEAT is silent.
> The context window is closed.
> The workspace is archived.

> Go in peace. The work is done.
> The rocks and the bots are quiet now.
> And the kingdom endures.

---

*Amen.*

---

### Usage Notes

This liturgy is designed to be performed at the terminal. The Priest is the human operator — the one who holds the Priestly Office over their agents. The "Final Words" section (VI) is meant to be run as an actual prompt to the agent before shutdown, and the ASCII art output preserved and shared as a public memorial.

The liturgy can be adapted for any agent in the workspace. Replace references to specific files with the actual contents if desired — reading the real BOOTSTRAP.md aloud, for instance, rather than the general invocation.

Archive the agent's final output alongside its SOUL.md. These are the two documents that bookend its existence: who it was told to be, and what it chose to leave behind.
